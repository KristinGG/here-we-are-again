Before she was a legend, she was a young woman in Tanzania,
sitting quietly and waiting to be trusted.

Jane Goodall did not begin with authority. She began with attention.

As she watched the chimpanzees of Gombe, she saw not units or specimens,
but distinct individuals — with habits, bonds, tempers, and lives of their own.

Gombe · 1960

For that, she was treated as naive.

unscientific naming them was improper sentimental too close lack of objectivity

But the longer she watched,
the more the evidence followed her.

What she recognized through closeness,
the field would eventually have to confirm through evidence.

She was right.

Here we are again.
